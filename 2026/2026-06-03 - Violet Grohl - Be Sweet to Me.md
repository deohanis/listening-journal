## General thoughts
*Be Sweet to Me* is the debut studio album by Violet Grohl. Exploring the rock landscape, Violet brings her own voice to the alternative scene, drawing inspiration from her father, Dave Grohl. Driven by heavy guitars and thrashing drums, the album contrasts raw intensity with her delicate, melodic vocals, shaping the foundation of her emerging signature sound.

On December 5, 2025, Violet released “THUM” and “Applefish”, announcing the same day on her social media. Since then, Violet has been working on music and experimenting with different media.

Three months later, Violet announces her debut album via social media, along with the album’s third single, “595”.

- Every track seems to be quite short.

## Track-by-Track Notes

### 1. THUM
**Themes:** 
- Nail biting addiction, anxiety

**Notes:**
- Addiction to nail biting that could be metaphorical for some kind of toxic relationship due to the bitter acetone taste when one bites their nails.

- The classic I'm quitting you... for now, like a temporary abstinence of that person/nail biting addiction.

- Resignation phase with "Why even take control?" simply accepting the addiction/toxic relationship.

- Brutal honesty that sometimes exists at the core of many toxic relationships, the fact that people that are stuck in them tend to gaslight themselves into thinking that it is right or that they like it that way, even when they know they don't.

- Somewhat simple chord progression but really groovy bass line section with the lead guitar.

- The Post-Chorus / Refrain kind of breaks the immersion.

- Love the vocal performance on the second (2) verse, Violet has a really good control of soft, velvety-like vocals.
  
- Seems to end abruptly when it could've been explored a bit more deeply.

**Score:** 60/100

### 2. 595
**Themes:** 
- Sexuality

**Notes:**
- Really impressive production with the layering rhythm, ringing and distorted guitars.

- I loved the distorted guitar riff contrasting the softness of Violet's vocal performance.

- The lead guitar layering the same notes Violet is performing was interesting.

- Again, the content of these two (2) songs seem to be rather shallow, one about nail bitting this one about an old t-shirt that had a sex telephone line.

- It works well as evoking that femme-fatale persona, sexual with hints of adventure and danger.

- Somewhat brings early 2000s nostalgia.

**Score:** 80/100

### 6. Mobile Star
**Themes:** 
- Social Media / mobile phone addiction

**Notes:**
- Dreamlike high freq bells mixed together with a really low synth and a 8bit style buzz.
- Has a sort of lullaby feeling to it.
- Every element makes this feel like you are laid in bed not doing anything, simply rotting away.
- The track develops into a much more hopeful version of itself after the midway point with the use of more uplifting chords.
- Conceptually makes perfect sense but the execution created something interesting but lacks in replay value.

**Score:** 40/100

### 7. Often Others
**Themes:** 
- Living in a dissociated, blame-filled headspace, chaotic emotional collapse.

**Notes:**
- Enjoyed the ending instrumental breakdown due to the massive pull it had with the usage of down tempo.
- Outside of the outro, nothing really paints the message that Violet Grohl tried to portray here.
- Would be interesting to keep the same vibe as the outro throught the whole song.

**Score:** 40/100

### 8. Applefish
**Themes:** 
- Feeling of falling behind, isolation, no safe space to return to, feeling of being unable to help yourself even when others try to help you. Self destruction.

**Notes:**
- Perfect continuation from 'Often Others' with the groovy heavy hitting breakdowns between vocal performances, perfectly contrasting each other with yet again the velvet-like vocals.
- Track progression into something softer and more relaxed.
- More of a laid back track that can be heard seamlessly, not much focus or interesting stuff going on at face value. 

**Score:** 50/100

### 9. Cool Buzz
**Themes:** 
- Being apart of something without being excluded like women in hardcore scene. Taking the front row.

**Notes:**
- Insane bass tone.
- Can't really enjoy the pre-chorus/chorus switch up, believe the track should keep itself more in the rest of the track's register.

**Score:** 60/100

### 10. Pool Of My Dreams
**Themes:** 
- Dissociation. Loss of time perception.

**Notes:**
- Complete 180 from the rest of the project with the digital ambient synths.
- There seems to be a high-pass filter on some of the sounds making the start of the track feel like its underwater while Violet Grohl's feel super clear kinda like a voice guide.
- Feels like the odd one out making it seem like it isn't part of the project.

**Score:** 40/100

### 11. Plastic Couch
**Themes:** 
- Artificial perfection, emotional repression, lack of true connection, lack of intimacy with people, feeling distant, emotional neglect

**Notes:**
- Finishing acoustic ballad.
- There was a sudden shift in vibe on the last 2.5 tracks. From the middle of 'Cool Buzz' onward.
- Felt really connected to the themes explored in this track.
- Love the transition back to the heavy instrumentals with loud cymbals to finish the project off after the realization that the illusion of perfection is starting to crack.

**Score:** 90/100

**Favorite Track(s):** Plastic Couch and 595
