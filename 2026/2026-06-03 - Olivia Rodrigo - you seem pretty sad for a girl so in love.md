## General thoughts
'you seem pretty sad for a girl so in love' is the third studio record by Olivia Rodrigo. Journey throughout her relationship with Louie, 1-7 tracks girl so in love segment, 8-13 tracks you seem pretty sad segment.

## Track-by-Track Notes

### 1. drop dead
**Themes:** 
- Falling in love

**Notes:**
- Instrumental breakdown feels quite weak, in terms of composition and mix/master.
- Ending of verse 2 felt a lot like those Disney soundtracks with the chord resolution
- Well accomplished Bridge section with a nice build up towards the Chorus, but it seems to again be quite weak
- Ending vocal performance was great with 

**Score:** 40/100

### 2. stupid song
**Themes:** 
- Finding true love, yearning

**Notes:**
- Beautiful melody on the Chorus.
- Again weird transition onto the 2nd verse
- Quick snappy bridge vocals contrasting with the Chorus
- Development of the instrumental throughout the song going from a simple piano to a mix of piano guitars, synths, etc

**Score:** 60/100

### 3. honeybee
**Themes:** 
- Difficulty showing ones emotions, fear of being let down/the other person leaving, finding peace or healing ones past wounds through a person, feelings of being grateful for that person showing up

**Notes:**
- Weird development from the last track, if the last track was flipped aka up-beat at the start developing into something softer to ease the transition into this track it would make more sense storytelling wise.
- Love the velvety like vocal performance on the first verse
- Great spatial atmospheric vibe with the back vocals, choir by Conan Gray, plucks layering over the piano keys.
- Great tension builder on the end of the first chorus with the plucks hitting in a more pronounced way on the second verse.
- The hopeful violin on the instrumental break works so well for releasing the tension at the end and going back to the calm bridge/chorus. 

**Score:** 90/100

### 4. maggots for brains
**Themes:** 
- Writers block, feeling incomplete when the other person is not there, as if nothing seems to be right.

**Notes:**
- Initial uneasiness due to the different 8bit esque instrumental.
- Nice way of developing the narrative
- Would prefer it going in a different direction instrumentally wise.
- Predictable and quite generic progression
- The whole maggots for brains concept feels quite interesting but the execution was quite poor and lacked depth, hope Olivia can explore this idea more on the following tracks.

**Score:** 20/100

### 5. u + me = <3
**Themes:** 
- Excitement for the new relationship, not caring for the bad things that could happen nor caring for what the other people say about love or relationships

**Notes:**
- The guitar tone really resembles old The Cure stuff / shoegaze like slowdive which is a really good thing
- Cool idea of having a second guitar playing root muted arpeggios on the background kinda like a bass.
- The melody picked for the Chorus clashes a bit with the theme previously showcased at the start of the case, doesn't quite work out for me.
- The melody gets better on the next sections like the second verse and the pre-chorus.
- The low pass tambourine crashing on the background makes everything feel like a weird xmas song

**Score:** 30/100

### 6. my way
**Themes:** 
- Standing her ground against a girl that seems flirtatious towards her boyfriend.

**Notes:**
- Siren-esque synths as if she is about to arrest someone
- Interesting muted guitar that is copying what the bass is going to do on the other sections of the song
- The Bridge fits perfectly with the Chorus
- Great background song for a payback track
- The first time she presents the chorus the transition feels a little forced with the pre-chorus, not as well executed as the Bridge idea. 

**Score:** 50/100

### 7. purple
**Themes:** 
- Transitional Track, lack of self while on a relationship

**Notes:**
- I like the idea that the project's switch track is called 'purple' due to the connotation in the music industry of the color blue with sadness and the color red being associated with anger, well, purple is the mix of both of those emotions/colors.
- Interesting addition of the organ piano melody to picture the falling of the relationship due to the instrument and the chord progression used being associated with funerals.
- Comparing physical childhood wounds to adult emotional wounds.
- I don't think the background beat with a high pass filter works well in this scenario
- Interesting idea of exploring obsession over someone until you feel like there is no 'you' left anymore, and you mix into the same (red + blue = purple) the title song.
- The odd beat I talked about resolves itself into a really pleasant instrumental on the second verse onward
- Background vocals on the chorus going up and down in pitch almost as if making a full circle, adding to the feeling of union, repetition, being stuck, etc.
- Being too attached that it leads to the demise of the whole relationship

**Score:** 80/100

### 8. the cure
**Themes:** 
- Insecurity, coming to terms that he was not the one aka the antidote

**Notes:**
- Intro highly influenced by Foo Fighters - Everlong chord progression, love it
- Love the acoustic performance
- The way Olivia resolved the Chorus worked perfectly contextually with the theme of the song
- Uplifting Major chords on the Chorus contrasting with the sad lyrics
- Really well executed with the mix of pads, synths, strings.
- Incredible spacious mixing.

**Score:** 100/100

### 9. begged
**Themes:** 
- desperately wanting more affection from her partner. Her requests just make her feel worse, knowing that instead of naturally being shown love, she has to beg for it.

**Notes:**
- Really well executed melancholic acoustic ballad.
- Slightly less interesting composition-ally, maybe so it does not take away from the lyrics
- Complete emotional exposure

**Score:** 80/100

### 10. what's wrong with me
**Themes:** 
- Feeling like something isn't right with yourself

**Notes:**
- Interesting melody on the Chorus, works well when Olivia and Robert complete each others tonality.
- Robert Smith's solo performance on the second verse wasn't quite up to par with my expectation maybe due to the style of the song he was coming in on.

**Score:** 40/100

### 11. less
**Themes:** 
- Sense of despair, loneliness, losing control, insecurity coming back to hurt you

**Notes:**
- Amazing vocal performance on the Chorus displaying Olivia's amazing vocal range
- Really great portrayal of the idea of love not being enough if it comes with painful side effects "If loving me means crying on the curb at LAX. Well, then I guess. I wish, I wish, I wish you loved me less" 

**Score:** 90/100

### 12. expectations
**Themes:** 
- Couldn't get over whatever that was

**Notes:**
- Feels really out of place compared to what the project sets up to be.
- Replays prior concepts used on previous hits like bad idea's radio like voice with the same rhythm.

**Score:** 10/100

### 13. cigarette smoke
**Themes:** 
- Closure, feeling that they lost time, not knowing why it went wrong

**Notes:**
- Pretty standard breakup song.
- Love the callback to honeybee and the whole cigarette smoke being something that is hard to get rid of the smell of, just like how hard it is for her to forget about him.

**Score:** 60/100
